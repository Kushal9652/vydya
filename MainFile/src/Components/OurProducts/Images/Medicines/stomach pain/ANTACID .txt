ANTACID :

Antacids are over-the-counter (OTC) medications that help neutralize stomach acid. Take antacids as directed to reduce the risk of diarrhea or constipation. They may also increase the chance of developing food sensitivities. In some cases, they can cause an allergic reaction.


H2BLOCKER :

Histamine H2-receptor antagonists, also known as H2-blockers, are used to treat duodenal ulcers and prevent their return. They are also used to treat gastric ulcers and for some conditions, such as Zollinger-Ellison disease, in which the stomach produces too much acid. In over-the-counter (OTC) strengths, these medicines are used to relieve and/or prevent heartburn, acid indigestion, and sour stomach.


ESOMEPRAZOLE :

Esomeprazole is used to treat conditions where there is too much acid in the stomach. It is used to treat duodenal and gastric ulcers, erosive esophagitis, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), and Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, a condition wherein the stomach produces too much acid.


PEPGRAM :

Peppermint Oil has been recommended for centuries by traditional herbalists for the support of healthy digestion. More recently, it has been found to support normal gastrointestinal function. Peppermint Gels are enteric coated to allow passage through the stomach and release within the intestines where it can be helpful in providing relief from occasional minor bowel discomfort. Suggested Usage- One capsule 3 times daily , taken 30-60 minutes before the food Ingredients- Enteric Coated softgel capsules contains: Peppermint oil ( Mentha Piperata) (Aerial part )- 0.2 ml


SIMETHICONE :

Simethicone is a medication used in the management and treatment of flatulence. This activity describes the indications, mechanism of action, and contraindications for simethicone as a valuable agent in the control of excess gas production.
